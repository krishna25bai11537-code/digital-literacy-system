# Stay Safe Online - Prevention Checklist

**Course:** CSE0001 - Digital Literacy  
**Task:** 5 - Part B  

---

A practical checklist for college students in India to protect themselves from cybercrime and online threats.

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts** - Whether it is your email, social media, or academic portal, always activate 2FA. Even if someone obtains your password, they cannot access your account without the second verification step.

2. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account** - Avoid using your name, date of birth, or simple sequences. A strong password combines uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Consider using a trusted password manager.

3. **Verify links before clicking** - Hover over or long-press any link to preview the actual URL before opening it. Fraudulent sites often use addresses that closely resemble legitimate ones (e.g., "vit-bhopal.net" instead of "vitbhopal.ac.in").

4. **Do not share OTPs with anyone - ever** - No legitimate bank, UPI platform, government body, or college authority will ever ask for your One-Time Password. Sharing it even with someone you trust is a security risk.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated** - Software updates often contain critical security patches. Delaying updates leaves known vulnerabilities open for attackers to exploit.

6. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities** - Public networks in cafes, hostels, or railway stations are frequently unsecured. Never log in to banking apps, college portals, or email accounts on public Wi-Fi without a VPN.

---

## UPI and Financial Safety

7. **Understand the difference between Send and Receive on UPI** - Approving a *collect request* on GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm means money leaves your account. To receive money, you only need to share your UPI ID — you never need to enter your PIN or approve any request.

8. **Never scan an unknown QR code to receive money** - QR codes on UPI apps are strictly for making payments, not for receiving them. If anyone tells you to scan a code to get money, it is a scam - stop immediately.

---

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

9. **Report any cybercrime immediately at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)** - India's National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal allows you to file complaints about financial fraud, cyberbullying, identity theft, and more. The sooner you report, the higher the chance of action being taken.

10. **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** - This is a 24x7 dedicated helpline for reporting cyber financial fraud in India. If you have been scammed online or via UPI, call 1930 immediately. Prompt reporting can help freeze fraudulent transactions before the money is withdrawn.

---

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Received suspicious UPI collect request | Decline immediately, do not enter PIN |
| Asked to scan QR code to "receive" money | Refuse — this is always a scam |
| Received unknown link via SMS or WhatsApp | Do not click, delete the message |
| Someone asking for your OTP | Hang up / stop responding immediately |
| Victim of online fraud | Call 1930 and report at cybercrime.gov.in |
