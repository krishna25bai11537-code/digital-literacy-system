# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Course:** CSE0001 – Digital Literacy  
**Task:** 4 – Part B  

---

## Do's

1. **Keep your profile professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that reflects your academic and career interests. Employers and internship coordinators often check social media.

2. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. **Use strong privacy settings** — Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information. Limit public visibility to only what you intend to share.

4. **Give credit where it's due** — If you share someone else's content, idea, or image, always acknowledge the original creator. This applies to academic work as well.

5. **Report harmful or abusive content** — Use the platform's reporting tools if you encounter cyberbullying, hate speech, misinformation, or inappropriate content. Don't stay silent.

---

## Don'ts

1. **Don't share your location publicly** — Avoid posting real-time location updates, check-ins, or photos that reveal where you live, study, or travel regularly. This is a safety risk.

2. **Don't post about others without consent** — Never share photos, videos, or personal details about classmates, friends, or faculty without their explicit permission.

3. **Don't engage with or spread misinformation** — Always verify news or claims before sharing. Forwarding unverified information — even unintentionally — can cause real harm.

4. **Don't use offensive or abusive language** — Comments made online are permanent and public. Avoid language that could be seen as disrespectful, discriminatory, or harassing.

5. **Don't overshare personal information** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, Aadhaar details, bank information, or any sensitive personal data on any platform.
